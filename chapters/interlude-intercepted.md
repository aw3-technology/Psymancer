# Interlude: Intercepted Transmission

**EMPIRE SIGNALS INTELLIGENCE DIVISION**
**INTERCEPT ANALYSIS, PRIORITY: ROUTINE**

**Analyst:** Lt. K. Hoffmann, SID Mediterranean Desk
**Intercept Source:** Wideband EM sweep, Mediterranean Sector 7-12
**Intercept Timestamp:** [Redacted]
**Signal Type:** Burst transmission, non-standard encoding
**Signal Duration:** 4.7 seconds
**Encryption:** None detected (see note below)

---

**ANALYST NOTES:**

Signal captured during routine sweep of known resistance communication corridors. Signal characteristics are inconsistent with standard resistance communication protocols. Forwarding analysis per standing orders.

**Signal content, raw transcription attempt:**

The signal consists of four components transmitted simultaneously on overlapping frequencies:

**Component 1:** Tonal pattern. Frequency range: 180-440 Hz. Non-standard intervals. Does not correspond to any known musical system or phonetic language. Pattern is rhythmic but the rhythm shifts; each repetition introduces a variation, as if the pattern is evolving in real time. Duration: full 4.7 seconds.

**Component 2:** Percussive pattern. Sub-sonic frequencies detected (below 20 Hz, felt rather than heard). Pattern is complex: polyrhythmic, with at least three independent rhythmic lines running simultaneously. Does not correspond to any known communications encoding.

**Component 3:** What appears to be a data stream encoded in the tonal pattern's overtone series. This is where it gets strange, sir. The overtones contain what our equipment reads as structured information, not random noise, not musical artifact, but organized data. However, the data does not correspond to any known encoding format. Binary analysis: negative. Hex analysis: negative. The structure is there, but the language it's written in is not in our databases.

**Component 4:** A carrier wave that does not carry anything our equipment can detect. The wave is present, the instruments register it, but its function is unclear. It may be structural: i.e., it holds the other three components in a specific relationship to each other, the way a frame holds the pieces of a window. Remove the carrier wave and the other three components become noise. With it, they become something. The analysts on my team are disagreeing about what.

---

**ANALYSIS:**

I've run this through the standard resistance communications decoder (SRCD v4.2) and the signal does not match any known resistance protocol. The resistance uses burst transmissions, yes, but encoded in standard digital formats: encrypted, compressed, designed for machine-to-machine interpretation. This signal is not designed for machine interpretation. It appears to be designed for — and I recognize this will sound non-standard in an intelligence report — it appears to be designed for *bodies*. The tonal and sub-sonic components are calibrated to produce physical responses in the listener: the chest, the hands, the area behind the eyes. I know this because I felt it. During playback, before I engaged my chip's audio normalization, I experienced 0.3 seconds of — the best description I can provide is pressure. Not painful. But present. As if the signal were pushing against something inside my chest that I had not previously been aware of.

My chip normalized the signal after 0.3 seconds and the sensation ceased. The normalized version of the signal is, as noted above, undecodable noise.

**Lt. Parisi, who also reviewed the signal, reports a similar physical response during the pre-normalization window. She describes it as "like remembering something I never learned." Her chip logged it as an anomalous somatosensory response and recommended no action.**

---

**CLASSIFICATION ASSESSMENT:**

The signal does not contain actionable intelligence in any format our systems can process. It does not contain coordinates, names, operational plans, or any other data relevant to Empire security. It does not correspond to any known resistance communication protocol.

**However:**

The signal was transmitted from a location within the known resistance operational area (Calabrian coastal region, approximate coordinates classified). The transmission timing correlates with resistance activity patterns in the area. And the signal's complexity — its four-component structure, its apparent design for physical rather than cognitive reception, its use of overtone-encoded data in a format no database recognizes — suggests a level of sophistication that is inconsistent with standard resistance communications capability.

My assessment: this is not a tactical communication. It is not an operational message. It does not contain troop movements or supply requests or the usual content of resistance signals traffic.

**It may be an emotional communication.** A message designed not to inform but to *sustain* — to transmit not data but presence, the way a voice on a phone call transmits not just words but the knowledge that the speaker is alive and speaking and here. If this is correct, the signal's content is not intelligence. It is something closer to a song.

**But songs, in the context of archaic resistance communities, are not merely songs. They are the medium through which non-phonetic consciousness is practiced and transmitted. A tonal signal of this complexity, if it is indeed a consciousness practice rather than a communication, suggests that the resistance is actively developing and distributing non-phonetic techniques via its communication infrastructure.**

This may be more significant than a coded message. A coded message moves information. This moves something else.

---

**RECOMMENDATION:**

No actionable intelligence detected. Signal filed under: CULTURAL ARTIFACT, NON-THREAT.

Recommend continued monitoring of this frequency corridor. If similar signals are detected, recommend analysis by the Cognitive Unity Division rather than Signals Intelligence, as the signal's function appears to be neurological rather than informational.
