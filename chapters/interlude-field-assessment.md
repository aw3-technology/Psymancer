**1. TARGET COMMUNITY OVERVIEW**

**Designation:** Archaic Community MED-7-C-01 (informal name: unrecorded; community does not use a fixed toponym in Empire Standard)

**Location:** Coastal hillside, southern Anatolian littoral. GPS coordinates classified. Terrain: Mediterranean scrub, olive cultivation, non-standard agricultural configuration (spiral and radial planting patterns; see Appendix C for satellite imagery). Access via unmarked trail from nearest Empire-registered settlement (Yalova village, 6.2 km).

**Population:** Estimated 50-60 individuals. Precise census unavailable due to non-registration status. No community members carry identification chips or monitoring patches. Community has evaded three previous census attempts (Ref: CUD-FI Operations Log, entries MED-7-A through MED-7-B, both classified ABANDONED due to operative extraction failure).

**Note:** Two previous operatives assigned to infiltrate this community were extracted without usable intelligence. Operative Marić (MED-7-A) reported "processing difficulties" after nine days and requested voluntary withdrawal. Operative Tanaka (MED-7-B) ceased transmitting after twelve days. Recovered seventeen days later in Antalya, disoriented, speaking a non-standard dialect. Currently undergoing neural recalibration at the Vienna facility. His file has been annotated: *Extended exposure to archaic consciousness practices may produce Phase Two chip destabilization in operatives with non-standard baseline profiles.* This assessment recommends Phase Three capability for the current operation.

These individuals are likely the community's knowledge carriers and represent the highest cultural-asset value for the Division's archive.

**4. INFRASTRUCTURE AND CAPABILITIES**

**4.1 Physical infrastructure:** Low-tech, mixed construction. Solar power (limited). Rainwater collection. Small medical clinic (salvaged Empire equipment, generation 1-2). No significant defensive capability. Warning system: tonal signal network (non-electronic, difficult to jam via standard countermeasures).

**4.2 Communications:** Single shielded uplink, long-range capability. Located near settlement center, poorly secured. Burst transmission protocol, estimated 3-4 communications per week with external resistance contacts.

**4.3 Defensive assessment:** Minimal. No conventional weapons detected. No fortification. Eastern approach unmonitored between 0200-0500 hours (satellite imagery confirms). Community's primary defense is geographic isolation and cultural illegibility: the settlement is designed to be unprocessable by chipped surveillance, not to resist direct action.

**6. RISK ASSESSMENT**

Operative risk: MODERATE. Previous operative failures suggest the community's practices may produce chip interference in susceptible individuals. Phase Three architecture is expected to mitigate this risk. However, extended exposure should be monitored via telemetry review at Division HQ.

Community risk to Empire: LOW (current), HIGH (projected). The community's advanced logographic practices, if successfully transmitted to the next generation, represent a persistent challenge to the Standard Linguistic Framework. Subject DARA's anomalous cognitive development is of particular concern: if her capabilities are reproducible, the community's educational model may need to be studied before it is neutralized.

**Recommendation:** Proceed with infiltration. Priority intelligence targets: (1) Subject Adaeze's resistance network connections; (2) Community's logographic writing system documentation; (3) Developmental assessment of juvenile subject DARA.
